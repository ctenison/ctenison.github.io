Abstract

As the lead research assistant for the MathBrain project at Stanford University, I contributed to studies on the effects of math tutoring on children's math ability. I collaborated with researchers to develop an 8 week math intervention tutoring program. We built experimental tasks, compiled assessment batteries and, using previous research and observations of young learners, modified and implemented the tutoring program. During my two years at Stanford Cognitive and Systems Neuroscience lab, we designed, piloted and ran this tutoring program with pre- and post-fMRI scans. Tutoring the children and analyzing the data from our study, we gained insight into the cognitive processes involved in learning mathematics in young children.
